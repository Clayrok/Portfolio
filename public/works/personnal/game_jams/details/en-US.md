# Game Jams

## Project Overview
Throughout my studies and professional career, I have actively participated in several Game Jams, developing unique titles under tight constraints. These experiences allowed me to iterate quickly on gameplay mechanics and take on diverse roles within small, agile teams.

## My Role: Generalist Developer & UI Specialist
In these collaborative environments, I focused on creating cohesive experiences by handling:
* **Gameplay Programming:** Developing core mechanics, player controllers, and AI systems.
* **UI/UX Implementation:** Designing and coding responsive menus and HUDs.
* **Game & Level Design:** Contributing to the conceptual phase and balancing mechanics.
* **Audio Integration:** Managing the import and implementation of sound effects and music.

## Challenges & Solutions

### 1. Extreme Time Constraints and "Scope Creep"
**Problem:** In a 48-to-72-hour window, it is easy to spend too much time brainstorming or trying to polish features before the core game is even playable.
**Solution:** I implemented a "Hard Deadline" rule for the ideation phase: if a final concept wasn't chosen by a specific hour, we moved forward with the best available idea. I also utilized rapid tracking tools (like Trello) to maintain team coordination. Technically, I adopted a "Layered Development" approach—focusing first on making features functional ("Making it exist") before attempting to "Make it pretty," ensuring a complete vertical slice was always ready by the submission deadline.

### 2. Fatigue and Diminishing Returns
**Problem:** Long hours and high-intensity coding often lead to burnout and a drop in creative quality over the course of a weekend.
**Solution:** To combat mental fatigue, I rotated tasks regularly, switching between UI logic, gameplay mechanics, and audio integration to stay engaged. I also advocated for scheduled rest periods; by prioritizing short, high-quality sleep over "powering through," our team maintained better code quality and fewer critical bugs in the final hours of the jam.
