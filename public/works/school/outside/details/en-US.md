# Outside

## Project Overview
Outside is a third-person stealth-action game developed as a final year project during my Gameplay Programming studies. Created over a 2-month period, the game follows a child with telekinetic powers attempting to escape a high-security laboratory. I served as the Lead Programmer for a team of three developers and three artists.

## My Role: Lead Gameplay Programmer
As the programming lead, I was responsible for the technical vision and the implementation of the core interaction systems entirely within Blueprints:
* **Telekinesis Mechanics:** Engineered the physics-based interaction system for environmental manipulation.
* **Character Controller:** Developed the player's movement set and stealth-focused controls.
* **In-Engine Cinematics:** Created a custom system for seamless narrative transitions.
* **Technical Leadership:** Orchestrated the programming team’s workflow and task distribution.

## Challenges & Solutions

### 1. Large-Scale Team Coordination and Production Management
**Problem:** With a six-person multidisciplinary team and a tight 2-month deadline, the project faced significant organizational risks, including task overlap and a lack of clear technical direction.
**Solution:** I stepped into the Lead Programmer role to formalize the production pipeline. I mapped out every technical requirement and evaluated tasks based on complexity and priority. By assigning features to team members based on their specific technical affinities and interests, I maximized the team's efficiency. I also acted as the primary technical liaison for the art team, ensuring their assets were integrated without performance bottlenecks.

### 2. Disparate Skill Levels Within the Programming Team
**Problem:** As a student-led project, the programming team had varying levels of experience with Unreal Engine and Blueprint optimization, which threatened the consistency of the codebase.
**Solution:** I implemented "Knowledge Sharing" sessions where we reviewed complex systems together. By mentoring my teammates on specific implementation patterns and Blueprint best practices, I elevated the overall technical standard of the group. This collaborative approach ensured that everyone could contribute meaningfully and that the final game felt like a cohesive product.
